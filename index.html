Graduate Capstone — TRST 760

Comprehensive estate plan for a blended, high-net-worth family

For my capstone, I served as the estate-planning associate for a hypothetical blended family holding a diversified ~$60M estate — a residence and vacation property, operating farmland, closely-held and publicly-traded stock, and a valuable inherited collection — with competing objectives across two spouses, children from a prior marriage, a disabled beneficiary, and a charitable goal.

I drafted a full estate plan and delivered a formal memorandum and presentation, coordinating tax minimization with the family's personal wishes. Representative techniques applied:

  • Marital & credit-shelter planning — structuring the unlimited marital deduction and bypass trusts to defer and reduce federal estate tax across both spouses' estates.
  • QTIP trusts — providing lifetime benefit to the surviving spouse while directing remainder interests (the vacation home and family farm) to the children, per stirpes.
  • Special-needs planning — a third-party special-needs trust to provide for a disabled adult beneficiary without disqualifying her needs-based SSI benefits.
  • Charitable strategies — gifting highly-appreciated stock and an inherited collection to a university, weighing outright gifts against a charitable remainder trust for income-tax and capital-gains efficiency.
  • Basis & income-tax planning — leveraging the step-up in basis at death and analyzing dividend and rental income streams from the farm, closely-held stock, and rental property.
  • Liquidity & titling — payable-on-death designations for immediate spousal access, and correcting asset titling (tenancy by the entirety, JTWROS, tenancy in common) to align with the plan.

Full memorandum available on request.
