Areas of Practice

Four disciplines, one duty:

The Master of Trust and Wealth Management prepares its graduates for four connected career paths. I bring a fiduciary mindset — shaped by military stewardship and entrepreneurial practice — to each of them.

Overview

Where I work

Trust and wealth management is not one job; it is four overlapping ones. A family's plan touches all of them at once — the trust that holds the assets, the plan that directs the cash flow, the tax strategy that preserves the value, and the portfolio that grows it. Below is how I think about each, and what I bring to it.

01

Trust Administration & Fiduciary Service

Trust Officer · Trust Administrator · Fiduciary Specialist

The trustee holds legal title to property that belongs, in every meaningful sense, to someone else — a surviving spouse, a child with special needs, a grandchild not yet born. The work is customized banking, investment, liquidity, credit, tax, and estate coordination for affluent clients, held to the highest standard the law recognizes.

  • Discretionary distributions, principal and income accounting, and beneficiary relations
  • Trust instrument interpretation and Regulation 9 fiduciary reviews
  • Administration of revocable, irrevocable, charitable, and special needs trusts
  • Estate settlement, court accountings, and agency accounts

What I bring: six years as an accountable Army officer answerable for $15M+ in property — meticulous documentation, internal controls, and a zero-loss record. Stewardship isn't a slogan to me; it's a discipline I've already lived.

02

Comprehensive Financial Planning

Financial Planner · Wealth Advisor · Private Client Advisor

A comprehensive plan turns a family's scattered accounts and goals into one coordinated strategy — covering cash flow, retirement, investments, risk and insurance, education funding, tax, and business succession. The value is not a product; it is translation and sequencing, so the essentials are always covered and no opportunity is left on the table.

  • Retirement income design, 401(k)/IRA strategy, and Roth conversions
  • Goal-based planning, scenario modeling, and plan presentation
  • Risk, insurance, and education-funding coordination
  • Business succession and buy-sell planning for owners

What I bring: a $2.2M first-year real estate practice built from zero — guiding families through the largest financial decisions of their lives, and managing the full client lifecycle from discovery to retention. See the lifetime plan in the Wealth Guide →

03

Estate & Tax Planning

Estate Planner · Wealth Strategist · Estate & Tax Specialist

Estate planning anticipates and arranges the disposition of an estate — maximizing what reaches the next generation by minimizing the taxes and expenses that erode it. It is the discipline where the largest numbers live, and where careful structure separates the family that planned from the family that reacted.

  • Wealth transfer strategy: marital deduction, credit shelter, QTIP, GRAT, and ILIT structures
  • Estate, gift, and generation-skipping transfer tax planning
  • Charitable strategies, basis step-up, asset titling, and beneficiary coordination
  • Estate settlement and fiduciary income tax (Forms 706, 709, 1041)

What I bring: graduate training across fiduciary, estate, and gift taxation, applied in a full capstone estate plan for a blended, high-net-worth family. See what settlement actually looks like in the Estate Guide →

04

Fiduciary Investment Management

Investment Manager · Portfolio Analyst · Trust Investment Officer

For a fiduciary, investing is not about chasing returns; it is about managing a portfolio prudently on behalf of someone who is trusting the process. The work guides asset management of securities and other assets to meet the goals set for institutional or private beneficiaries — under the Prudent Investor standard, with discipline and documentation.

  • Asset allocation, investment policy statements, and rebalancing
  • Equity and fixed-income analysis, diversification, and risk-tolerance calibration
  • Performance reporting, benchmark analysis, and liquidity management
  • Prudent Investor Rule compliance and investment-committee process

What I bring: disciplined resource forecasting learned planning logistics for a 5,000-person brigade — matching supply to need, managing risk in advance, and answering for the result.
